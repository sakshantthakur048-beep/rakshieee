About The Ledger

Most career advice is either a motivational poster or a spreadsheet. We wanted neither.

The Ledger started as a question asked in group chats and exit interviews: was it actually worth it? Not the version people tell at parties — the real arithmetic of salary, commute, growth, and the quiet cost of starting over.

We built a calculator that does the math honestly, including the part people forget — unvested 401(k) money left behind. And we built a place to read what real people decided, in their own words, filed next to the numbers that shaped the decision.

No company names. No recruiters in the comments. Just the record.

Have a story to add, or numbers that don't match what we assumed? Tell us.
